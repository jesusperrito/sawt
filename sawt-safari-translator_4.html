the four ideas that sell the magic

★ the heart

characters, not species

every reply comes from a written somebody — a name, an age, a history, a running joke. it's the difference between reading a fact about lions and being teased by one in particular. the whole product hangs off this.

a

point to talk

the camera recognises who's in frame and locks the conversation to that exact animal — and dual-capture puts you in the shot too. point, and they speak.

b

they know the moment

time, heat, feeding, snoozing or pacing — the reply reacts to reality. the same question at noon and at dusk gets two different answers, so a second visit is a brand-new show.

c

arabic + english, natively

not a buried setting — both languages are first-class in voice and in text, because this is dubai and the audience is the world.

why the park loves it
dwell time

the map and the camera turn the whole park into a hunt for characters. visitors walk further and stay longer chasing the next one to meet.

repeat visits

because the characters react to time and weather, it's never the same conversation twice — the night-safari lion is a different animal entirely.

education

a fact wrapped in a personality beats a fact on a plaque. learn mode carries the conservation story in first person, where it lands instead of preaches.

free reach

dual-capture clips of you and a talking animal are made to be shared — every visitor leaves with a film of the character that spoke to them, by name.

the easy way in

launch it as a talking-booth at one habitat first

one enclosure, one crowd-favourite character, one physical activation at the railing — point, ask, and hear them answer out loud. it proves the magic, shoots its own launch film, and de-risks the full app.
